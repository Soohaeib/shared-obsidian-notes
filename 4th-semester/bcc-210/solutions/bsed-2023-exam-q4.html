(a)

Definition of Poverty and Poverty Alleviation Programs

Poverty refers to a multi-dimensional state or condition in which an individual or a community lacks the financial resources and essential conditions to enjoy a minimum standard of living. It is generally categorized into two dimensions:

  • Absolute Poverty: Occurs when human beings lack the absolute basic necessities for survival, such as adequate food, safe drinking water, sanitation, clothing, shelter, health, and education.
  • Relative Poverty: Occurs when people do not enjoy the average standard of living compared to the rest of society, reflecting income inequality and unequal distribution of resources.
Poverty Alleviation Programs of Different Organizations in Bangladesh

In Bangladesh, poverty alleviation is driven by a collaborative approach involving government entities, Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs), and specialized microfinance institutions.

Government Initiatives (Social Safety Net Programs - SSNPs)

The Government of Bangladesh operates comprehensive social safety nets managed by various ministries:

  • Food Security Programs: Initiatives like Vulnerable Group Development (VGD) and Vulnerable Group Feeding (VGF) distribute food grains to marginalized, food-insecure households, especially during natural disasters or lean seasons.
  • Employment Generation Programs: Schemes such as the Employment Generation Program for the Poorest (EGPP) and Test Relief (TR) provide short-term employment opportunities in infrastructure development for the rural poor.
  • Social Allowances: Cash transfer programs including the Old Age Allowance, Allowances for Widows, Deserted and Destitute Women, and Financial Assistance for Disabled Persons target structural vulnerabilities directly.
Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs) and Microfinance Institutions (MFIs)
  • BRAC (Building Resources Across Communities): Globally recognized for its Ultra-Poor Graduation Program, BRAC utilizes a time-bound, sequenced approach that provides asset transfers (such as livestock), enterprise training, and healthcare to help families permanently graduate out of extreme poverty.
  • Grameen Bank: Founded by Nobel Laureate Professor Muhammad Yunus, it revolutionized poverty alleviation through the Microcredit Model. By offering collateral-free collateral microloans targeted predominantly at rural women, it fosters self-employment, structural financial independence, and rural entrepreneurship.
  • ASA (Association for Social Advancement): Focuses on providing highly cost-effective, sustainable microfinance services to small entrepreneurs and low-income families to ensure liquidity for small-scale income-generating activities.

(b)

Brief Note on the 8th Five-Year Plan of Bangladesh

The 8th Five-Year Plan (FY2021 – FY2025) of Bangladesh, titled "Promoting Prosperity and Fostering Inclusiveness," served as the foundational operational strategy to implement the country's Perspective Plan 2021–2041, aiming to transition Bangladesh towards an upper-middle-income nation.

Key Objectives and Focus Areas
  • Economic Growth and Resilience: The plan targeted an ambitious average GDP growth rate of around 8.0%, prioritizing macroeconomic stability and a rapid post-COVID-19 pandemic economic recovery.
  • Poverty Reduction: It aimed to aggressively lower the absolute poverty rate to 15.6% and drop the extreme poverty rate to 7.4% by the end of the fiscal year 2025.
  • Job Creation and Industrialization: Focus shifted toward modern, export-led manufacturing and diversifying industrial sectors outside of Ready-Made Garments (RMG) to generate formal sector employment for the massive incoming youth demographic.
  • LDC Graduation Preparedness: Strategically outlined defensive economic reforms to absorb the shocks associated with Bangladesh's official graduation from the Least Developed Country (LDC) category, focusing on losing preferential trade benefits (GSP) by fostering bilateral trade treaties.
  • Sustainable Development and Green Growth: Integrated climate change resilience frameworks aligned with the Delta Plan 2100, promoting renewable energy, sustainable urban expansion, and environmental preservation.

(c)

Sustainable Development and the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

Sustainable Development is defined as development that meets the needs of the present generation without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own operational needs. It is built upon the integration of three core, interdependent pillars: Economic Growth, Social Inclusion, and Environmental Protection.

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

Adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015 as part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, the SDGs comprise 17 global goals with 169 specific targets designed to eliminate poverty, protect the planet, and guarantee peace and prosperity by 2030.

Goal Number Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) Core Intent
SDG 1 No Poverty Eradicate extreme poverty and establish robust social protection floors globally.
SDG 2 Zero Hunger End hunger, achieve food security, improve nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture.
SDG 3 Good Health and Well-being Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all individuals at all ages.
SDG 4 Quality Education Ensure inclusive, equitable, quality education and lifelong learning opportunities.
SDG 5 Gender Equality Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls.
SDG 6 Clean Water and Sanitation Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all.
SDG 7 Affordable and Clean Energy Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy.
SDG 8 Decent Work & Economic Growth Promote sustained, inclusive economic growth, full employment, and decent work.
SDG 9 Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure Build resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive industrialization, and foster innovation.
SDG 10 Reduced Inequalities Reduce income and opportunity inequalities within and among countries.
SDG 11 Sustainable Cities & Communities Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable.
SDG 12 Responsible Consumption & Production Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns across industries.
SDG 13 Climate Action Take urgent action to combat climate change and its multi-sectoral impacts.
SDG 14 Life Below Water Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources.
SDG 15 Life on Land Protect, restore, and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems and forests.
SDG 16 Peace, Justice & Strong Institutions Promote peaceful societies, provide access to justice, and build accountable institutions.
SDG 17 Partnerships for the Goals Strengthen global implementation mechanisms and revitalize global partnerships.
